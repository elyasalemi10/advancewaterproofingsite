Sealant vs Caulking

Understanding the right material for joints and movement

Key Differences

  • Caulks: low-performance, low-flexibility materials for static, interior joints.
  • Sealants: high-performance, elastomeric materials (e.g., silicone, polyurethane) for dynamic, exterior joints with significant movement.

Common Failures When Using the Wrong Material

  • Adhesion loss and joint leakage
  • Cracking or cohesive failure under thermal movement
  • Discoloration and UV degradation

Best Practice

  • Use backer rod and correct width-to-depth ratios
  • Prime where required and follow manufacturer specs
  • Select elastomeric sealants for exterior movement joints
